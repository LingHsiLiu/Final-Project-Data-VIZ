In Pie Charts, it shows total unemployed population and unemployment rate for US States from 2011 to 2015. The blue color is employment rate and the red color is unemployment rate. The total US unemployment rate in 2011 is 9%. In 2015, it is droped down to 5.3%. In pie charts, we will easily to know unemployment rate is going down and the economic became better in United States.

The unemployment rate is calculated as the number of unemployed people divided by the number of people in the labor force. In general, unemployment rate is shown as an indicator of economically backward because hiring or dismissing employees usually consume manpower and resources, thus, companies will reduce working time when the economy experience recession. Then, companies will consider dismiss more employees when the situation become worst. In the same way, when the economy rebound and companies will increase working time as respond, they will expansion employees when the there is prosperity.
